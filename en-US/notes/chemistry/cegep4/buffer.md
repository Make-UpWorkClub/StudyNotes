# buffer

Solution that resists change in pH

Composed of a [[acid|weak acid]] and its conjugate base or a [[en-US/notes/chemistry/cegep4/base|weak base]] and its conjugate acid

> [!abstract] buffering capacity
> Capacity to resist pH change
> Increases with concentration of weak acid / base and conjugate base / acid

## Examples

> The dissociation constant of propanoic acid (HC3H5O2) is $1.3 \cdot 10^{-5}$. What is the pH of a buffer made with 0.50 M propanoic acid and 0.25 M NaC3H5O2 (the sodium salt of propanoic acid)?

$$
\begin{align}
K_a & = \frac{[\ce{H^+}][\ce{A^-}]}{[\ce{HA}]} \\
1.3 \cdot 10^{-5} & = \frac{x(0.25 + x)}{0.50 - x} \\
\approx 1.3 \cdot 10^{-5} & = \frac{0.25x}{0.50} \\
x & = 2.6 \cdot 10^{-5}\pu{M} \\
 \\
pH & = -\log 2.6 \cdot 10^{-5} = 4.59
\end{align}
$$

> Calculate the pH of 0.500 L of a buffer solution composed of 0.50 M formic acid (HCO2H) and 0.70 M sodium formate (HCO2Na) before and after adding 10.0 mL of 1.00 M HCl.

$$
\begin{align}
K_a & = 1.8 \cdot 10^{-4} \\
 \\
K_a & = \frac{[\ce{H^+}][\ce{A^-}]}{[\ce{HA}]} \\
1.8 \cdot 10^{-4} & = \frac{x(0.70 + x)}{0.50 - x} \\
\approx 1.8 \cdot 10^{-4} & = \frac{0.70x}{0.50} \\
x & = 1.3 \cdot 10^{-4}\pu{M} \\
 \\
pH & = 3.89 \\
 \\
[\ce{H^+}] & = \frac{1.00 \cdot 0.0100}{0.500 + 0.0100} \approx 0.020\pu{M} \\
 \\
K_a & = \frac{[\ce{H^+}][\ce{A^-}]}{[\ce{HA}]} \\
1.8 \cdot 10^{-4} & = \frac{(1.3 \cdot 10^{-4} + 0.020 + x)(0.70 + 1.3 \cdot 10^{-4} + x)}{0.50 - 1.3 \cdot 10^{-4} + x} \\
\approx 1.8 \cdot 10^{-4} & = \frac{(1.3 \cdot 10^{-4} + 0.020 + x)(0.70 + 1.3 \cdot 10^{-4})}{0.50 - 1.3 \cdot 10^{-4}} \\
x & = −0.020 \\
 \\
pH & = 3.89
\end{align}
$$
