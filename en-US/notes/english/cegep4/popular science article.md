# popular science article

- Purpose:
	- To inform and entertain the public
	- To promote science
- Emphasizes context and analysis (importance for the layman)
- Language:
	- Mixes narration with information
	- Uses delayed revelation to create intrigue
	- Sensational
	- Uses literary devices such as imagery, contrast, personnification, quotes, statistics to attract attention
	- Uses first person pronouns for intimacy
	- Uses second person pronouns for engagement
	- Simple vocabulary
- Transparency rather than objectivity
- Be fair by only covering reliable claims; don't strive to be balanced by covering both sides
- Issues:
	- Sensationalism affects accuracy
