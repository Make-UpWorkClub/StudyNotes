# science fiction

[[Literary genre]]
Part of speculative fiction

- Demands extensive scientific knowledge from the author
- High standards for accurate, up-to-date science
- Uses scientific method to examine hypothetical changes
	- `E.g.` the Three Laws are tested against the laws of nature by introducing variables
- Variation on common topics
	- `E.g.` alien invasion, but the aliens have different forms and intents

## Categories

### Science as subject

Science as theme, subject and plot
`Aka.` hard science fiction

- Science as character
- Experimentation of ideas
- Frequent time travel

### Science as plot

Science drives the plot

- Focus on extrapolation
- Frequent gadgets

### Science as background

Science everywhere in the story, but not the focus

- Story can often be retold in another setting.

### Science as metaphor

Science as concrete form of intangible ideas and emotions
Alternatively story as symbol of science

- `E.g.` the falling apart of a housewife's life is representation of the workings of entropy.
- `E.g.` corporate politics illustrates the insect world.
- Frequently science also as subject and plot
- Frequent exploration of present-day problems in the future
