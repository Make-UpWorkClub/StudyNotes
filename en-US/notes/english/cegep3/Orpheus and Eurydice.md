# Orpheus and Eurydice

Greek myth

## Characters

### Orpheus

- Poet, musician, prophet
- Lyre player
	- => idealistic
	- => liar
- Son of the muse Calliope
- One of Jason's Argonauts
- Ruhl: idealistic, black and white

### Eurydice

- Wife of Orpheus
- Daughter of Apollo in some texts
- Hadestown: strong
- Ruhl: independent

### Ruhl: Father

- Servant of Eurydice
	- <= comparison to a porter

## Motifs

### Ruhl: water

- Orpheus goes in the ocean to sing.
	- Symbolizes the underworld
	- Symbolizes peace
- Drinking water
- Rain in the elevator to the underworld
	- Symbolizes forgetfulness and forgetting of love
- Rain in which Eurydice's father heard her name
	- Symbolizes remembrance
- Thirst
	- Symbolizes desire

### Ruhl: string

- Ring made of string
	- Symbolizes Eurydice becoming an instrument
	- Symbolizes Orpheus' control over Eurydice
	- Symbolizes remembrance of love
- Room made of string
	- Symbolizes remembrance
	- Symbolizes cage
- Symbolizes fate

### Ruhl: interest

- Interesting book
- Interesting Man
- Uninteresting Orpheus

## Topics

### Love

- Love is both selfless and selfish.
- Love cannot endure death.
- Hadestown & Ruhl: Love is defeated by survival needs or money.
	- <= Ruhl: Eurydice wants a gold ring instead of a string ring.
	- <= Ruhl: Eurydice is preoccupied of money when arrives at the underworld.
- Love is a possessive restriction.
- Love between daughter and father precedes love between husband and wife.
	- Eurydice chooses father over husband

### Ruhl: Knowledge

- Ignorance makes one unable to empathize with others.
	- Dead people are childish: the Child, Eurydice, the Stones
- Forgetfulness requires remembrance.

### Death

- Reason of death:
	- Virgil: Eurydice falls into a nest of vipers while escaping a satyr.
	- Aeschylus: Orpheus is torn apart by Maenads for spurning the gods.
	- Atwood: Orpheus is torn apart while singing as praise and defiance.
	- Some versions: Orpheus commits suicide to rejoin Eurydice.
- Atwood & Ruhl: death is peaceful.
	- <= Ruhl: dead people are compared to stones.
