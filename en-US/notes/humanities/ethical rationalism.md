# Ethical rationalism

Branch of [[duty ethics|deontology]] formalized by Kant

- Reason sets us free
	- => we're duty bound to follow reason
	- => action should reflect will
- **Imperative**: justification of the action (command)
	- Categorical: universal
		- The only categorical imperative: act only if the rule that applies to the action can be logically applied to everyone ("Act only on that maxim which you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law")
		- Duty must be categorical to apply to everyone.
	- Hypothetical: conditional
- Rational beings have absolute worth => should treat each other as ends in themselves, `i.e.` not use each other as tools
- Objects have conditional worth (dependent on will).
- Disadvantages:
	- Intelligence of certain animals
	- Questionable rationality of children
	- May be exploited to discriminate against a group deemed irrational, `e.g.` women and slaves
