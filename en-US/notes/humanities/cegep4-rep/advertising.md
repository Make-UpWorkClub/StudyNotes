# advertising

> [!abstract] product placement
> Ad for a product in another form of media, e.g. an actor using a product in a film

> [!abstract] native advertising
> Ad concealed into another form of media (manipulating the reader into reading)

## Rules

- Do not lie about the product or its benefits
- Do not promote harmful outcomes
- Treat the consumer with respect
- Do not undermine consumer agency
- Protected as free speech
- Medical advertising illegal in western countries except in US
	- Reminder ads allowed in Canada

## Advantages

- Makes products cheaper through economy of scale
- 

## Problems

- Subversion of rational thinking and threat to autonomy
	- Emotional manipulation
	- Manipulation of truth
		- Possibility of lying by omission
		- Greenwashing
	- Spreading of dissatisfaction
		- Of not owning the product, `e.g.` FOMO
		- Of social position and identity
			- `E.g.` unachievable body standards create permanent dissatisfaction with the body
			- => insecurity and stress
	- => creation of false needs and irrational spending
		- => combines with credit cards to cause debt
		- => encourages immediate gratification
- Inevitability
	- => exposure to children and other vulnerable groups
	- => undermines consumer agency
- Placement in questionable locations, `e.g.` religious buildings and schools
- Bad influence
	- Promotion of materialism
	- Promotion of selfishness
	- Promotion of racism, sexism, bigotry and stereotypes
	- Promotion of sex, gambling and drugs
- Cultural appropriation
- "Buyoff" of politically unsatisfied people
	- => undermines democracy

## Categories

### Informative

Only information

- Generally considered ethical

> [!example] Costco sale flyers

### Persuasive

Uses persuasion techniques

#### Benefit

(Often exaggerated) explanation of the benefits to the user

#### Fear

Explanation of bad outcomes from not using product

#### Identity

Solidification of identity through community belonging

> [!example] Ram Super Bowl ad
> Associates the truck with masculinity

#### Symbol

Association / representation of the product with symbols

- Not irrational to pay for a symbol for its meaning

> [!example] Ram Super Bowl ad
> Associates truck with masculine symbols like going outdoors, chainsaw and dragon
