# Enthalpy

Total heat of a system
[[State function]]

$$
H = E + PV
$$

At constant pressure:

$$
\Delta H = \Delta E + P\Delta V = \Delta E + RT\Delta n_g
$$

## Standard enthalpy of formation

Heat change when one mole of a particular compound is formed from its elements in standard states 
`Sym.` $\Delta H_f^\circ$

$$
\Delta H_{f_{element}}^\circ = 0
$$

## Bond enthalpy

Heat change to break a particular bond in one mole of gas
Measure of molecular stability
`Sym.` $BE$

> [!info] Bond enthalpies of diatomic molecules are directly measurable, thus they have more significant figures.

## Standard enthalpy of reaction

Heat change of a particular reaction
`Sym.` $\Delta H_{rxn}^\circ$

$$
\Delta H_{rxn}^\circ = \sum_{i\in \text{reagents}} n_i\Delta H_{f_i}^\circ - \sum_{i\in \text{products}} n_i\Delta H_{f_i}^\circ
$$

For gases:

$$
\Delta H_{rxn}^\circ = \sum_{i\in \text{reagent bonds}}BE_i - \sum_{i\in \text{product bonds}}BE_i
$$

> [!example] Write the equation for the formation of $\ce{MgCO_3_{(s)}}$.
> $$
> \ce{2Mg_{(s)} + 2C_{(graphite)} + 3O_{2(g)} \to 2MgCO_3}\, \Delta H_{rxn}^\circ = 2\Delta H_f^\circ
> $$
