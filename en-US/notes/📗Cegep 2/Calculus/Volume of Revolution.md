# Volume of Revolution

Application of the [[definite integral]]

Let $f$ be the function rotated around the $z$ axis, then

$$
V = \int_{a}^{b} A(x) \, \mathrm{d}x = \int_{a}^{b} \pi f^2(x) \, \mathrm{d}x
$$

## Examples

> Find the formula for the volume of a sphere.

$$
\begin{align}
A & = \int_{-r}^{r} \pi f(x) \, \mathrm{d}x \\
 & = 2\pi \int_{0}^{r} f(x) \, \mathrm{d}x \\
 & = \int_{0}^{r} \sqrt{r^2 - x^2} \, \mathrm{d}x
\end{align}
$$

Let $x = r\sin \theta$, then $dx = r\cos\theta d\theta$.

$$
\begin{align}
 & = 2\pi \int_{0}^{r} r^2\cos^2\theta \, \mathrm{d}\theta \\
 & = 2\pi r^2 \int_{0}^{r} \frac{1}{2}(1 + \cos2\theta) \, \mathrm{d}\theta \\
 & = \pi r^2\left[ \theta + \frac{\sin 2\theta}{2} \right]_{0}^{r} \\
 & = \pi r^2\left[ \arcsin x + \frac{\sin(2\arcsin x)}{2} \right]_{0}^{r} \\
 & = \pi r^2\left( \arcsin r - \arcsin 0 + \frac{\sin(2\arcsin r)}{2} - \frac{\sin(2\arcsin 0)}{2} \right) \\
 & = \pi r^2\left( \arcsin r + \frac{2\sin(\arcsin r)\cos(\arcsin r)}{2} \right) \\
 & = \pi r^2(\arcsin r + r\sqrt{1 - r^2})
\end{align}
$$

> Find the formula for the volume of the solid generated by rotating the region bounded by $y = x^2$ and $y = 3x$. about 1. the x axis, 2. the y axis.

The two lines intersect at $(0, 0)$ and $(3, 9)$.

x axis:

$$
\begin{align}
A & = \pi \int_{0}^{3} (9x^2 - x^4) \, \mathrm{d}x \\
 & = \pi\left[ 3x^3 - \frac{x^5}{5} \right]_0^3 \\
 & = \pi\left( 3\cdot3^3 - \frac{3^5}{5} \right) \\
 & = 32.4\pi
\end{align}
$$

y axis:

$$
\begin{align}
A & = \pi \int_0^9 \left( y - \frac{y^2}{9} \right) \, \mathrm{d}y \\
 & = \pi \left[ \frac{y^2}{2} - \frac{y^3}{27} \right]_0^9 \\
 & = \pi\left( \frac{9^2}{2} - \frac{9^3}{27} \right) \\
 & = 13.5\pi
\end{align}
$$

$$
\begin{align}
A & = \pi \int_{0}^{4} ((\sqrt{y} + 1 - 6)^2 - (1 - 6)^2) \, \mathrm{d}y \\
 & = \pi \int_{0}^{4} (y - 10\sqrt{y}) \, \mathrm{d}y \\
 & = \pi\left[ \frac{y^2}{2} - \frac{20y^{\frac{3}{2}}}{3} \right]_0^4 \\
 & = \pi\left( \frac{4^2}{2} - \frac{20\cdot4^{\frac{3}{2}}}{3} \right) \\
 & = -\frac{136}{3}\pi
\end{align}
$$
