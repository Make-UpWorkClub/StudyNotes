# Antiderivative

The function $F(x)$ is an antiderivative of $f(x)$ if $F'= f$.

> [!important]+
> Antiderivatives are not unique. $F(x) + c$ is the **general antiderivative** of $f(x)$.
> If $g(x)$ and $h(x)$ are both antiderivatives of $f(x)$, then $g(x) = h(x) + c$.
